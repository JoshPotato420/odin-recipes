Sweet and Savory Tocino

Tocino is a cured pork dish popular in Filipino cuisine. It's enjoyed for breakfast, often paired with garlic fried rice and eggs. This recipe creates a sweet and savory tocino with a delicious balance of flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 kilogram pork loin or kasim, cut into thin strips
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • tablespoons white vinegar
  • tablespoon black peppercorns
  • cloves garlic, minced
  • teaspoon ground paprika (optional)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Pork: In a large bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, white sugar, vinegar, peppercorns, garlic, and paprika (if using). Add the pork strips and toss to coat evenly. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 8 hours, or preferably overnight.

  2. Cook the Tocino: Heat a pan over medium heat. Arrange the pork strips in a single layer and cook for 3-5 minutes per side, or until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess oil as needed.

  3. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the tocino hot with garlic fried rice and eggs for a delicious Filipino breakfast. You can also enjoy it on its own or in sandwiches.
